A robot with evolved morphology and controller

Published: Wed 02 April 2014
By Ian Macinnes

In Artificial Intelligence.

My doctorate back in 2006 was partly research on evolving the morphologies of robots and controllers in simulation and then constructing them. Most of the robots are now lost but today I discovered these movies of a single example.

The fitness function of the population of robots was simply to move. The population size was 30. The robot uses a continuous time recurrent neural network as a controller, co-evolved with the morphology over 1000 generations. This was the fittest agent from the final generation:

This is the agent after it was physically constructed:

An interesting aspect is the curved arms that were evolved to allow the robot to roll over, then roll back.
