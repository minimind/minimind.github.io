Virtual creatures gallery 1 of 10

Published: Sun 08 August 2010
By Ian Macinnes

In Artificial Intelligence.

I'm collecting videos of animats whose morphology and controllers have been evolved in order to improve my own video (and animat) making ability. I'm looking for good visual designs and agents that move naturally, even though they may not have a very lifelike body shape. I'll start with the granddaddy of them all - virtual creatures created by Karl Sims.

These were the first virtual creatures developed, way back in 1994 and I suspect still the most visually striking. They use a simple developmental algorithm for the morphology and a unique controller, a hybrid of neural network and various functions to generate different activation patterns that are used to control the body.

He also evolved creatures to compete against each other. The agents do not have much in the way of sensors and so this was really a different evolutionary pressure for movement rather than coordinating perceptual and action.
